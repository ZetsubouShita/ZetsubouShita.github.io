Introduction

Jim Davis is a Full Professor in the Dept. of Computer Science and Engineering. He received his Ph.D.from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

RESEARCH: His main research areas include Computer Vision, Artificial Intelligence, and Machine Learning with a focus on image/video analysis and automated surveillance. He has received over $3M in research funding (to date) from the National Science Foundation (including a prestigious CAREER award), U.S. Air Force, Los Alamos National Lab, U.S. Army Night Vision Laboratory, Intel, and Ohio Board of Regents.

TEACHING: Prof. Davis offers classes in Computer Vision (CSE 5524) and Artificial Intelligence (CSE 5052) at Ohio State University. The Artificial Intelligence course was specifically designed to fill a need and outreach to the large body of non-CSE students interested in the topic.

SERVICE: In addition to his Full Professor duties and service in the professional societies, he also serves in the CSE Dept. as the Coordinator for Undergraduate Research, the Chair of the Computer Committee, and a Member of the Executive Committee.

Directions to My Office

Finding your way to The Ohio State University is easy. It is in the center of Columbus, slightly north of downtown.

From the North:

Take any major highway to I-270. Take I-270 to SR 315 south. From SR 315 south, exit at Lane Avenue.

From the South:

Take any major highway to I-71 north. Take I-71 north to SR 315 north. Exit at Lane Avenue.

From the West:

Take any major highway to I-70 east. Take I-70 east to SR 315 north. Exit at Lane Avenue.

From the East:

Take any major highway to I-70 west. Take I-70 west to SR 315 north. Exit at Lane Avenue.

From John Glenn International Airport:

  1. Take I-670 west to I-71 south.
  2. Take I-71 south to I-70 west.
  3. Take I-70 west to SR 315 north. Exit at Lane Avenue.
  4. Take Lane Avenue (going east into OSU) over the bridge until you reach Tuttle Park Place road.
  5. Take a right onto Tuttle Park Place road and continue down (shortly) through the big 4-way intersection (intersecting with Woody Hayes Dr.).
  6. Shortly after the intersection you will see a large parking garage on your left (with an immediate entrance into the garage from the road).
  7. The OSU Stadium will be directly on your right. Enter this garage and take the ticket to park your car (we will give you a pass later to exit the garage at no charge).

I am located in office 491 Dreese Labs, directly adjacent (behind) to the parking garage. It is the tallest red brick building in the immediate area. It also has a characteristic white "spool" on the very top of the building.
